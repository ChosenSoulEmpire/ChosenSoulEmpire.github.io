Crisis Hotlines

Certainly! If you or someone you know is in crisis, there are several hotlines available in Florida to provide immediate help and support. Here are some options:

  1. 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline: This confidential, free crisis service is available 24/7. You can call or text 988, or chat online at 988lifeline.org. The Lifeline connects you to the nearest crisis center in the national network, offering crisis counseling and mental health referrals.
  2. Crisis Text Line: Text “HELLO” to 741741. This hotline is available 24/7 throughout the U.S. and connects you with a crisis counselor who can provide support and information.
  3. Veterans Crisis Line: Call 1-800-273-TALK (8255) and press 1, or text to 838255. This free, confidential resource is available to veterans 24/7, even if they are not registered with the VA or enrolled in VA healthcare.
  4. National Disaster Distress Helpline: Call or text 1-800-985-5990. This helpline provides immediate crisis counseling for emotional distress related to natural or human-caused disasters
  5. National Domestic Violence Hotline: Call 1-800-799-7233 or text “LOVEIS” to 22522.
  6. National Child Abuse Hotline: Call 1-800-4-A-Child (1-800-422-4453) or text 1-800-422-4453.
  7. National Sexual Assault Hotline: Call 1-800-656-HOPE (4673) or use the online chat.

Remember that if you or someone else is in immediate danger, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room. These hotlines are here to provide support and connect you with resources during difficult times.
